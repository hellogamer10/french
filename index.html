Les prépositions et les adverbes de lieuPrepositions and adverbs of placePointers: The title means words that help you describe where something is.
Les contraires / Les antonymesOpposites / The antonymsPointers: These are pairs of words that mean the reverse of each other.
pour / contrefor / againstPointers: These are opposites, similar to agreeing for something versus being against it.
devant / derrièrein front of / behindPointers: If you are devant a desk, you are facing it; if you are derrière it, you are at its back.
sur / souson / underPointers: The book is sur the table (on the table), or sous the table (under the table).
près de / loin denear / far fromPointers: Près de means close, while loin de means a long distance away.
à gauche / à droiteto the left / to the rightPointers: These are used for directions.
Verbes en ayer, oyer, uyerVerbs ending in ayer, oyer, uyerPointers: These verbs are grouped together because they follow a specific spelling rule when you change them to match 'I,' 'you,' 'he,' etc..
payer (to pay), nettoyer (to clean), essuyer (to wipe)to pay, to clean, to wipePointers: These are examples of verbs that follow this pattern.
Je paie, Tu paies, Il/Elle paieI pay, You pay, He/She paysPointers: Notice that in the Je, Tu, Il/Elle forms, the 'y' changes to an 'i' in the spelling for payer.
These are not irregular in conjugation but they have a radical change in je, tu, il and ils forms of the present tense.Pointers: This means the end of the verb changes (conjugates) normally, but the root (the 'radical') changes its spelling slightly (like the 'y' changing to 'i') for certain subjects.
Other verbs: envoyer, balayer, appuyerOther verbs: to send, to sweep, to pressPointers: These other verbs also follow the same spelling change pattern.
French Term / PhraseEnglish TranslationExplanation for a Class 8 Student
entrebetweenThis is used when something is in the middle of two other things, like standing between two friends.
chezat/to someone's placeThis word is special! It usually means "at the house of" or "at the business of" a person (e.g., chez le docteur - at the doctor's).
àat, toThis is a very common word used to show location (at school) or direction (to the park).
dansin, intoThis means inside something, like a pencil in the box.
defrom, ofThis often shows origin (from India) or possession (of the book).
depuissince, forThis word is used for time. It tells you when an action started, like "I have been studying since morning".
verstowardsThis shows direction, meaning movement in the direction of something else.
pendantduringThis is used for time, meaning throughout a specific period, like during the class.
au bout deat the end ofThis describes a location at the finish point, like a shop at the end of the street.
au coin deat the corner ofThis shows a specific point where two things meet, like meeting at the corner of the building.
autour dearoundThis means surrounding or encircling something, like people standing around a table.
en face dein front of, oppositeThis means being directly opposite something, or facing it.
à côté denext toThis means right beside something.
au centre dein the center ofThis means right in the middle of a space or area.
au bord deat the edge ofThis refers to being at the very limit or rim of something, like at the edge of the water.
au milieu dein the middle ofThis is similar to au centre de, meaning halfway through or surrounded by.
en dehors deoutside ofThis refers to a location that is not inside a defined area.
jusqu'àuntil, up toThis sets a limit in time (until noon) or distance (up to the gate).
parby, throughThis can show how something happens (e.g., traveling by bus) or motion through a space.
